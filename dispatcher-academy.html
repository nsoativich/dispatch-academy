Kim bu dispatcher?
INTRO

Dispatcher — truck driver (carrier) bilan freight broker o'rtasidagi ko'prik. Sen driverning nomidan brokerlarga qo'ng'iroq qilasan, yuk topasan, narx kelishasан, hammasini koordinatsiya qilasan. Toshkentda o'tirib, Amerikaning highway'larida "virtual" ishlaysan. Daromad: har bir load'dan $150–$300 komissiya.

Dispatcher vs Broker — farqi nima?
MUHIM
DISPATCHER
  • → Driver tomonida ishlaydi
  • → Litsenziya shart emas
  • → Driver shartnoma tuzadi
  • → Komissiya: 5-10% load'dan
  • → Boshlash osonroq
BROKER
  • → Shipper tomonida ishlaydi
  • → MC litsenziya kerak ($300+)
  • → O'zi kontrakt tuzadi
  • → Margin: $200-800/load
  • → Ko'proq kapital kerak

Muomala Scripts
Real so'zlar, real holatlarga. Yodla, mashq qil, o'zlashtrір.
Broker'ga birinchi qo'ng'iroq
COLD CALL
SEN
"Hi, this is [Ism] calling on behalf of [Carrier Company Name]. I'm their dispatcher. I was looking at your load posted on DAT — the one going from Chicago to Atlanta, load number [XXXX]. Is that still available?"
Doim load raqamini tayyor tut. Bu professional ko'rsatadi.
BROKER
"Yes, it's available. What do you have?"
SEN
"We have a 53-foot dry van, available tomorrow morning in Chicago. Driver has clean record, no violations. We're ready to go. What's the rate on that?"
Truck ma'lumotini aniq ayt. "Clean record" — broker uchun muhim.
BROKER
"We're at $2,200."
SEN
"I appreciate that. Looking at current market rates for this lane, we were thinking closer to $2,500. Is there any flexibility there?"
"I appreciate that" — polite, lekin firm. Market rates reference — mantiqiy argument.
Narx kelishish — advanced taktika
NEGOTIATION
BROKER
"Best I can do is $2,300. That's it."
SEN
"I understand. Let me check with my driver real quick and call you right back."
Hech qachon darhol "yes" dema. Bu taktika — 2-3 daqiqa sabr qil, qayta qo'ng'iroq qil. Broker pressure ostida ko'proq beradi.
SEN
"Hey, I'm back. My driver said he can make it work at $2,400 — that's his final number. He's a great driver, always on time, no claims. You won't have issues with him."
Driver'ning ijobiy xususiyatlarini ayt — bu added value. Ko'pincha broker rozi bo'ladi.
Truck breakdown — crisis management
EMERGENCY
DRIVER
"Hey man, my truck broke down. I'm on I-40, somewhere near Oklahoma City."
SEN
"Okay, stay calm. Are you safe? Is the truck off the road? Good. I'm calling the broker right now, I'll handle everything."
Birinchi — driver'ni tinchlantir. Keyin broker.
SEN
"Hi [Broker Name], this is [Ism] from [Company]. I need to give you a heads up — our driver experienced a mechanical issue on I-40 near OKC. We're handling it right now, getting roadside assistance. ETA may be delayed by 3-4 hours. I wanted to notify you immediately and I'll keep you updated every hour."
Transparent bo'l, darhol xabar ber. "Every hour update" — bu professional ko'rsatadi va broker'ni tinchlantiради.
Yangi driver bilan birinchi uchrashuv
CARRIER
SEN
"Hey [Driver Name], I'm [Ism], I'll be your dispatcher. My job is simple — I find you loads, negotiate the best rates, handle all the paperwork and communication. You drive, I handle everything else."
Driver'ga value'ingni aniq ayt. "You drive, I handle everything" — bu powerful pitch.
SEN
"My fee is 8% per load. So if I book you a $3,000 load, I take $240. You keep $2,760. I only make money when you make money — so it's in my interest to get you the best rates possible."
Fee'ni ochiq ayt. "I only make money when you make money" — trust o'rnatadi.
E'tirozlarni hal qilish
OBJECTIONS
BROKER
"We already have preferred carriers for this lane."
SEN
"I totally understand. Many brokers say that, and I respect it. But preferred carriers aren't always available when you need them. I'd love to be your backup option — and once you work with my driver, I think you'll move us to the top of your list."
"Backup option" — bu foot-in-the-door taktikasi. Kamtarona, lekin strategic.
BROKER
"Your rate is too high."
SEN
"I hear you. What rate works for you? I want to make this work. But I also need to make sure my driver covers his fuel and expenses — he's got a family to feed. Can we meet somewhere in the middle?"
"Family to feed" — emotional, lekin effective. Broker'ni insonga aylantiradi.

USA'da veteran truck driverlar juda katta segment — va ular loyalty'ga qaraydi, pulga emas. Agar sen "veteran-friendly dispatcher" bo'lsang, word-of-mouth marketing o'zi ishlaydi. Facebook'da veteran truckers guruhlar to'la. Bitta yaxshi driver = 5 ta referral. Hech qaysi Uzbek dispatcher bu niche'ni olmagan.

Toshkentdan "Timezone Advantage" strategiyasi
Toshkent vaqti USA East Coast'dan 9 soat oldinda. Demak — sen ertalab 7:00 da ishga tushsang, USA'da kechki 22:00. Night shift loadlar cheap va ko'p bo'ladi. Sen USA dispatcher'lar uxlab yotganda aktiv bo'lasan. Brokerlar senga qayta-qayta qo'ng'iroq qilishadi, chunki sen "always available" bo'lasan. Bu superpower — lekin hech kim ishlatmaydi.
